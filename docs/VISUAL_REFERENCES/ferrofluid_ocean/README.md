# Visual References — Ferrofluid Ocean

**Family:** fluid
**Render pipeline:** ray_march + post_process
**Rubric:** full (gated by V.6 certification)
**Last curated:** 2026-05-13 by Matt (V.9 redirect; see DECISIONS.md D-124)

> **Stylization caveat for any session reading this folder.** Every photograph in this folder was sourced because it teaches a *specific* trait — material behavior, scale, lattice character, swell motion, palette anchor, atmosphere, or lighting mechanic. None of them is a faithful rendering of "Ferrofluid Ocean as it should appear." Read each image only for the trait its annotation calls out, and *actively disregard* every other property of the image — particularly: studio gel lighting colors; the literal cause of any natural phenomenon depicted (auroras, storms, sun-on-water); foreground objects (lotus veins, foam crests, beach rocks, lightning bolts, mountain silhouettes, lake bottoms, celestial point sources, distant shorelines); any palette that conflicts with §10.3.5's "distant fog cools to dark purple" intent. The preset's hue comes from (a) mood-valence-tinted IBL ambient per **D-022** and (b) the moving colored beam rig per §5.8 / §10.3.6, not from any of these photographs. Direct-lit albedo is fixed by §4.6: `float3(0.02, 0.03, 0.05)`. Per-image disregard guidance follows the convention promoted to rule level in **D-065 §(c)** (`SHADER_CRAFT.md §2.1` step 2).

## Mandatory traits (per SHADER_CRAFT.md §12.1)

For this preset specifically, the following implementations are mandatory:

- [ ] **Detail cascade:**
  - **macro** = hex-tile spike lattice (`hex_tile` from `Utilities/Geometry`) with `spike_height ∝ stems.bass_energy_dev` per §4.6 + §10.3.1, *composed on top of* a Gerstner-wave macro displacement field driving the base height of the lattice (4–6 superposed Gerstner waves; amplitude routed from arousal + drums_energy_dev accent per the Audio routing notes below); the *scope* of the lattice extends to an ocean-portion horizon, not a contained dish (see `01_*` for scale, `02b_*` for swell motion geometry)
  - **meso** = domain-warped spike-center positions via `warped_fbm` per §3.4 + §10.3.2; flow velocity driven by `stems.drums_beat` rising edges (see `02_*`); peak-energy ripple density on excited surface per `02c_*`
  - **micro** = `fbm8(p * 15.0)` normal perturbation at amplitude `0.02`; hash-lattice micro-droplets at spike tips on high amplitude per §10.3.3 (see `03_*` for surface grain, `03b_*` for tip droplet behavior)
  - **specular** = `mat_ferrofluid` from §4.6 with anisotropic roughness aligned to spike axis per §10.3.4 (see `04_*`, dual hero reference for specular and lighting)
- [ ] **Hero noise function(s):** `fbm8` (8-octave, ≥ rubric minimum), `warped_fbm` (domain-warp for lattice defects), and a hash-lattice for micro-droplet placement. All from `Shaders/Utilities/Noise/` (V.1). **Gerstner-wave displacement is implemented preset-level** per the §4.14 `mat_ocean` recipe comment convention ("Gerstner-wave displacement and capillary ripples are SDF/geometry concerns at the preset level"); no Gerstner utility exists in the V.1 noise tree, and V.9 implements the Gerstner sum inline as part of its `sceneSDF`.
- [ ] **Material count and recipes:** `mat_ferrofluid` (§4.6, primary spike material) + `mat_ocean` (§4.14, between-spike fluid base reading deep absorption — preserved under redirect for the body-of-liquid depth implication that Gerstner motion makes more prominent) + **thin-film interference layer** via `thinfilm_rgb` from `Utilities/PBR/Thin.metal` (promoted from §12.3 catalog-wide strongly-preferred to per-preset mandatory under the V.9 redirect; provides the "hint of blue in highlights" called out in §4.6's material comment and gives beam reflections a subtle angular shift that reads as "magnetic fluid" rather than chrome; see §4.18 `mat_chitin` for a reference call-site pattern, though V.9 calls `thinfilm_rgb` directly with its own parameters). `mat_ferrofluid` and `mat_ocean` from `Shaders/Utilities/Materials/` (V.3); thin-film from `Shaders/Utilities/PBR/` (V.1). Plasma-family exemption does **not** apply; full 3-material requirement holds.
- [ ] **Audio reactivity (D-026 deviation primitives only):**
  - `stems.bass_energy_dev` → spike height (Rosensweig field strength)
  - `stems.drums_beat` → beat-surface ripple + lattice flow velocity (rising edges, accent only)
  - `stems.vocals_energy_dev` → spike sharpness / surface tension (lower = blunter)
  - `stems.other_energy_dev` → rotational flow direction of domain-warp seed
  - Arousal (5s low-pass) + `stems.drums_energy_dev` accent → macro Gerstner swell amplitude (NEW under redirect; arousal sets sustained baseline at ~70%, drum energy adds short-period crest emphasis at ~30%)
  - Arousal → stage-rig beam orbit speed (NEW under redirect)
  - `stems.vocals_pitch_hz` normalized inline (perceptual log-scale over 80 Hz–1 kHz; confidence-gated at `stems.vocals_pitch_confidence >= 0.6`) with `stems.other_energy_dev` fallback → stage-rig beam color rotation (NEW under redirect; same `palette()` phase-shift pattern as MV-3c, per §5.8). `vocalsPitchNorm` from MV-3c is no longer available — it was retired in DSP.2 S9.
  - `stems.drums_energy_dev` → stage-rig beam intensity envelope (NEW under redirect; envelope, not edge-trigger)
  - **No absolute-threshold patterns** (`f.bass > 0.22`, etc.) per D-026 — these are explicitly disallowed. FeatureVector fallbacks below.
- [ ] **Silence fallback (D-019 warmup):** at `totalStemEnergy == 0` the preset must still render. Crossfade via `smoothstep(0.02, 0.06, totalStemEnergy)` from FeatureVector proxies (`f.bass_att_rel` for spike height, `f.beat_bass` for ripple, `f.mid_att_rel` for surface tension, `f.treb_att_rel` for flow direction) to true stem routing. ## Expected traits (per §12.2 — at least 2 of 4)

- [ ] **Triplanar texturing on non-planar surfaces** — applicable: yes. Spike surfaces are strongly non-planar; uniplanar would stretch the micro-detail noise on near-vertical spike walls. Use `triplanar_normal` from `Utilities/PBR/Triplanar.metal` for the §10.3.3 surface-scale detail.
- [ ] **Detail normals** — applicable: yes. Combine the macro spike-shape normal with the §10.3.3 `fbm8(p * 15.0)` micro-detail normal via `combine_normals_udn` from `Utilities/PBR/DetailNormals.metal`. Without this the spikes look injection-molded.
- [ ] **Volumetric fog or aerial perspective** — applicable: yes. §10.3.5 specifies "distant fog cools to dark purple." Use `scene_fog` set against the §10.3 atmosphere intent (see `07_*` for the static-tint character — fog is *material*, not phenomenon); tint via D-022 mood-valence so negative valence biases the fog cooler/deeper-purple.
- [ ] **SSS / fiber BRDF / anisotropic specular** — applicable: yes. §10.3.4 calls for anisotropic reflection aligned with spike axes. The spike's tangent direction (radial outward from spike center) becomes the anisotropy axis. (See `04_*` for the dual specular-and-lighting anchor; see `08_*` for how a diffuse colored gradient reads as anisotropic across a dark reflective body at landscape scale.)

All four expected traits apply. Target ≥ 3 of 4 implemented for headroom against the rubric.

## Strongly preferred traits (per §12.3 — at least 1 of 4)

- [ ] **Hero specular highlight visible in ≥60% of frames** — applicable: yes. The §4.6 material at `roughness = 0.08` produces near-mirror highlights, and the §5.8 stage rig is positioned specifically to put highlights on spike tips from the camera angle (see `04_*` for the beam-on-dark-metal mechanic). This should be free given the lighting recipe.
- [ ] **Parallax occlusion mapping** — applicable: no. POM is for surface-displacement on flat-ish surfaces (bark, concrete walls). The spike lattice already provides 3D displacement via the SDF; layering POM on top would compound cost without visible benefit at this material's roughness.
- [ ] **Volumetric light shafts or dust motes** — applicable: optional. Could add faint above-surface dust motes catching the orbiting §5.8 stage-rig beams as atmospheric texture, but the scene is intentionally dark-dominant — defer unless the scene otherwise reads empty.
- [ ] **Chromatic aberration or thin-film interference** — *promoted to mandatory under the V.9 redirect* (see Mandatory traits → Material count and recipes above). Retained as a list entry for traceability; not double-counted in the §12.3 minimum.

Hero specular alone satisfies the §12.3 minimum; thin-film's promotion to mandatory leaves three remaining §12.3 candidates with only hero specular applicable.

## Anti-references (failure modes specific to this preset)

What a Claude Code session is most likely to produce by accident; what this preset must NOT look like:

- **Generic chrome metaballs / smooth reflective blobs** with no spike character. The single most likely failure mode if the session shortcut to writing a Beer's-law or simple-displacement surface instead of implementing the §4.6 hex-tile spike field. See `05_anti_chrome_blob_AIGEN.jpg`.
- **Perfectly regular hexagonal lattice.** Skipping the §3.4 domain warp on spike centers produces a math-pattern look rather than a magnetic-fluid look. The defects in `02_meso_lattice_defects.jpg` are not optional. A specific risk if the session over-reads `01_*`, which incidentally appears regular at its sampling scale.
- **Radial / hub-and-spoke spike arrangement.** A specific risk if the session over-reads the lotus-leaf vein structure in `03b_*`. Spike centers are hex-tile-distributed, not radial. The lotus is in the folder *only* for the droplet behavior at micro scale.
- **Foam-capped water as the between-spike base.** A specific risk if the session over-reads the upper portion of `06_*`, the foam crests at the top of `02b_*`, or pattern-matches generic "ocean" imagery. The §4.6 between-spike surface is *mirror-metal*, not water with surf; ferrofluid does not foam.
- **Active weather effects in the fog.** A specific risk if the session over-reads `07_*` and reproduces lightning, dramatic flicker, or rolling cloud movement. The §10.3.5 fog is a static atmospheric tint — tone, not phenomenon.
- **Static surface / missing Gerstner swell.** A specific risk if the session implements the spike-lattice height modulation but forgets the underlying macro Gerstner displacement. The surface must move as a coherent body of liquid, not have spikes pulsing on a flat plane. The new `02b_*` is the reference for what's missing; absence of swell motion at any time other than total silence is a documented failure.
- **Stage lighting reads as a club rig** rather than a physical lighting setup. Moving colored beams that strobe on beat onsets in saturated party colors, or beam motion that is jittery and beat-quantized rather than continuous and slowly orbital. The §5.8 beam rig produces continuous palette-driven hue rotation and slow orbital motion (beam orbit speed ← arousal, hue ← `vocals_pitch_hz` normalized inline with `other_energy_dev` fallback, intensity ← `drums_energy_dev` envelope) — never beat-strobed direction changes or onset-edge intensity pulses. See `04_*` for the *quality* of beam-on-ferrofluid (one frozen instant of physical beam light); the failure mode is beam *behavior* (jittery on-beat strobes, oversaturated party palette).
- **Point-source light reflections / vertical pillar reflections on the surface.** A specific risk if the session reads the bright celestial point in `08_*` as a directive about preset lighting. The §5.8 stage rig produces continuous diffuse gradients across the surface, not sharp point reflections forming vertical pillars (like a moon or lighthouse reflecting in a calm lake). Pillar reflections are exactly the wrong reading.
- **Calm state still showing spikes.** A specific risk if the session ties spike height to `bass_energy_dev` but doesn't crossfade the lattice itself out at total silence. The destination silence state is `10_silence_calm_body.jpg` — a smooth body of liquid with macro swell only, *no spike lattice present*. "10% baseline" is the prior spec and is retired under the redirect.
- **Soft / diffuse highlights.** Anything above `roughness = 0.15` reads as plastic. The §4.6 `roughness = 0.08` is non-negotiable for the "razor-sharp" character. See `04_specular_razor_highlights.jpg`.
- **Saturated direct-lit color.** The preset is not red, not purple, not any color in albedo. All visible color comes from IBL reflection + D-022 mood-tint on ambient + §5.8 beam rig reflections. Direct albedo is `float3(0.02, 0.03, 0.05)`. The colored gels in `02_*` and `04_*` are studio photography, not palette directives.
- **Single-octave fBM in the spike field.** Failed Approach §35. The §4.6 `fbm8` jitter is the floor; lower octave counts produce visibly procedural lattice spacing.
- **Beat-driven primary motion.** Per `CLAUDE.md §Audio Data Hierarchy`, beat onsets are accents only; continuous energy (`*_energy_dev`) is the primary driver. A preset where spike height pumps only on `drums_beat` rising edges fails the "feels locked to the music" test. Same rule extends under the redirect to swell amplitude (arousal-baseline, drums-accent) and beam intensity (drums_energy_dev envelope, not drums_beat) — energy envelopes, not onset edges.

## Audio routing notes

Specific audio→visual mappings that must hold (cite D-026 deviation primitives and D-019 stem warmup):

- **Spike height ← `stems.bass_energy_dev`** (continuous, primary). Fallback: `f.bass_att_rel`. Crossfade via `smoothstep(0.02, 0.06, totalStemEnergy)`. This is the Rosensweig "field strength" parameter; it must respond to bass envelope, not bass beats.
- **Beat-surface ripple ← rising edges of `stems.drums_beat`**. Fallback: `max(f.beat_bass, f.beat_mid)` (failed approach #26 — single-band keying misses snare-driven tracks). Ripple is an *accent* added on top of the continuous lattice motion, not the primary driver.
- **Spike sharpness / surface tension ← `stems.vocals_energy_dev`**. Fallback: `f.mid_att_rel`. Lower vocal energy → lower surface tension → blunter, broader spike profiles. Higher vocal energy → sharper, narrower spikes.
- **Rotational flow direction ← `stems.other_energy_dev`** (accumulated as a slow rotation of the domain-warp seed offset). Fallback: `f.treb_att_rel`. Slow drift, not jittery.
- **Macro Gerstner swell amplitude ← arousal (5s low-pass baseline) + `stems.drums_energy_dev` accent** (NEW under V.9 redirect). Arousal sets a sustained baseline amplitude that responds to overall musical intensity; `drums_energy_dev` adds a smaller short-period crest emphasis on top. The accent contribution must stay smaller than the baseline so the swell does not read beat-pumped; targeted ratio is baseline ≈70% / accent ≈30% at full energy. The independence of swell amplitude and spike height is load-bearing — calm-body-with-spikes and agitated-body-without-spikes must both be reachable states.
- **Stage-rig beam orbit speed ← arousal** (NEW under V.9 redirect). 5s low-pass smoothing. Maps a slow continuous orbit rate; at silence beams continue orbiting at minimum speed in default palette. No beat-coupling on motion direction or speed.
- **Stage-rig beam color rotation ← `stems.vocals_pitch_hz` normalized inline with `stems.other_energy_dev` fallback** (NEW under V.9 redirect). Same `palette()` phase-shift pattern as `vl_pitchHueShift()` from MV-3c, scaled to ±0.20 for visible chromatic motion via perceptual log-scale normalization over 80 Hz–1 kHz (no precomputed `*_norm` field — `vocalsPitchNorm` was retired in DSP.2 S9). Confidence gating at `vocals_pitch_confidence >= 0.6` per MV-3c. For instrumental tracks where vocals are absent, `other_energy_dev` carries the harmonic-content-density accent layer (substituted for `other_centroid_dev`, which does not exist — `StemFeatures` only has `*_energy_dev` deviation primitives, not centroid deviations).
- **Stage-rig beam intensity ← `stems.drums_energy_dev`** (NEW under V.9 redirect). Envelope-tracking, not edge-triggered; beams brighten and dim with drum energy continuously rather than strobing on drum onsets. Per-light intensity smoothed with 150ms τ to prevent jitter.
- **Distant fog hue ← D-022 mood valence**. Negative valence → deeper purple, cooler. Positive valence → warmer purple drifting toward magenta. Fog tint multiplies IBL ambient per `RayMarch.metal` `iblAmbient *= scene.lightColor.rgb`, so the shift is visible across the whole scene, not just direct-lit pixels. (See `07_*` for the static atmospheric character — fog is *material*, not phenomenon.)
