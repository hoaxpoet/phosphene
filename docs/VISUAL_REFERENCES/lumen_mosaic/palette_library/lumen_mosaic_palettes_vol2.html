Six further palettes

Continuation of the library. Six new plates drawing from sources the first seven did not touch — Indian festival, mineral geology, mid-century American painting, tropical biology, Persian manuscript, Japanese woodblock.

Each was chosen against an explicit audit of which primary colour territories were missing from Vol. I.

Audit · what Vol. I did not cover Pure citrine yellow (not gold or amber) · true scarlet (not blood-red or crimson) · chartreuse and peridot · amethyst at saturation · ochre and burnt sienna at saturation · coral that is neither hot magenta nor blood-red · pistachio at saturation · Prussian blue (greener and deeper than ultramarine) · Persian turquoise (distinct from Caribbean) · willow and cypress greens.
08

Holi

Indian spring festival. Powdered gulal pigments thrown skyward — the air at the festival peak. Five distinct pinks span ultra-saturated gulal through rose; the signature is the pink–turmeric–vermilion–Krishna-blue contrast no other palette achieves. Brings banana yellow (a clean primary the library has been missing) and neem (saturated leaf-lime).

09

Geode

Gemstone cross-sections laid out on a museum drawer. Each colour a distinct mineral. Brings citrine (the pure yellow the library has lacked), amethyst at full saturation, peridot chartreuse, malachite, rose quartz as a saturated rose (not pastel), and tiger’s eye for the warm-brown register at jewel-tone chroma. Hematite and smoky quartz are the deep-anchor cells.

10

Rothko Chapel

Late Rothko, Houston 1971. Deep saturated planes — oxblood, plum, aubergine, ochre, burnt sienna, rust. The first palette here that achieves vividness through depth rather than brightness. The whole library’s “low-value high-chroma” pocket. A panel pulling from this reads as meditation, not motion. Either exactly right or exactly wrong for the preset, depending on the playlist — but adds territory nothing else covers.

11

Tropical Aviary

Scarlet macaw, toucan beak, quetzal tail, Morpho butterfly, cock-of-the-rock, heliconia flower. Pure primary saturation at biological extreme. Brings true scarlet (cleaner and more orange-leaning than blood-red), pure yellow, parrot lime, and heliconia coral — the library’s first warm pink that is neither hot magenta nor crimson. Closest analogue to Carnival but with biological rather than folk-art register.

12

Persian Miniature

Safavid manuscript painting, 16th century — Tabriz court, Shiraz, Isfahan. The canonical Persian palette is famously narrow but deeply saturated: lapis ground stone, malachite, vermilion, saffron, gold leaf. Brings Persian turquoise (a specific darker blue-green distinct from Caribbean cyan), pistachio, Persian carmine, and pomegranate. The most disciplined of the new six — hues cluster, range is depth not breadth.

13

Ukiyo-e

Edo woodblock printing — Hokusai’s Great Wave, Hiroshige’s Tōkaidō Road. Prussian blue arrived in Japan in the 1820s and reshaped late Edo printmaking; it is unlike any blue elsewhere in the library — greener and deeper than ultramarine, more saturated than indigo. Pair with willow green, susuki gold, sakura cerise, and Hokusai turquoise for the seasonal register. The brown anchors (sumi, tea) read as paper texture and ink wash.
